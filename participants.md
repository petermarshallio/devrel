# Participants

The ecosystem graph has nodes. But not all nodes participate in the same way.

Three types of participant make up the ecosystems in which DevRel operates.
Understanding which type a practitioner is — at any given moment — matters
enormously to designing programs that actually serve them. The wrong program for
the wrong participant type is not just ineffective: it can erode the trust that
makes the ecosystem function.

## [Beneficiary](participants/beneficiary.md)

Receives from the ecosystem — capability, confidence, solutions to problems.
Most practitioners are Beneficiaries most of the time, and that is exactly as it
should be. Beneficiaries represent the ecosystem's reach: every person who gets
value from it without giving anything back yet is a future Donor waiting for the
right moment.

## [Donor](participants/donor.md)

Contributes to the ecosystem without expecting a direct return on any specific
act. Answers the question. Writes the tutorial. Shows up to the hackathon with
something to give, not just something to build. Donors are the force multipliers:
their contributions extend the ecosystem far beyond what any DevRel team could
reach alone.

## [Guardian](participants/guardian.md)

Both Donor and Beneficiary simultaneously — structurally load-bearing in the
graph. The Guardian's participation is what makes an ecosystem resilient rather
than merely active. When they leave, they take edges with them. When they write,
others cite them. When they speak, the room changes.

---

Most practitioners move between these types over time — and sometimes occupy more
than one simultaneously. A Beneficiary who finds themselves wanting to give back
is already becoming a Donor. A Donor whose contributions become load-bearing in
the graph is becoming a Guardian.

DevRel's role is not to categorise practitioners, but to create the conditions
in which that movement flows naturally.
