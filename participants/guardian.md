# Guardian

**What they give and receive:** Everything — simultaneously.

The Guardian is Donor and Beneficiary at once. They give back because they care
about the ecosystem as a whole, not just their own problem. They receive because
the ecosystem they help sustain continues to serve them. These two directions do
not create tension — they reinforce each other.

This is the hat-wearing expert who gives back. The practitioner who has been
around long enough to remember when the ecosystem needed more of them, and who
has decided to be that person for the next generation.

## Their role in the graph

Guardians are the load-bearing nodes. Remove them and the graph does not just
get smaller — it becomes less connected. Their edges are strong, bidirectional,
and distributed. They connect practitioners to each other, to knowledge, and to
the organisation in ways no individual DevRel effort can replicate or replace.

In graph terms, Guardians are high-degree nodes with strong connections in both
directions. When they move organisations, they carry their relationships with
them — because those relationships matter to them personally, not instrumentally.

The [maturity model](../maturity/README.md) treats Guardian emergence as the
critical signal of transition from a Dependent ecosystem to a Contributing one.
Their growth, retention, and health are the most reliable leading indicators of
ecosystem trajectory.

## What they look like in practice

- Answers questions without being asked, and follows up to check the answer
  actually helped
- Has written content the ecosystem cites — not just reads, cites
- Shows up to the roadmap session with prepared opinions and follows up when
  something changes as a result
- Brings other practitioners into the ecosystem because they genuinely believe
  it will serve them
- Is already known by practitioners who have never met them

## Why they matter more than any other participant type

The Guardian's departure is expensive in a way that a Beneficiary's or even a
Donor's is not. When a Guardian leaves, they take edges with them. Practitioners
who relied on their answers now look elsewhere. The knowledge they held becomes
unavailable. The trust they represented evaporates.

That is why earning Guardian trust is the highest-stakes relationship in the
ecosystem. And why treating Guardians as a marketing channel — or as validators
of decisions already made — destroys exactly the thing that makes them valuable.

## Where they appear in the programs

[Involvement programs](../programs/3-involvement.md) are substantially built
around Guardians. The Guardians program exists specifically to identify, recognise,
and sustain them — to make their contribution feel consequential and their
investment in the ecosystem feel honoured.

[Innovation programs](../programs/4-innovation.md) give Guardians genuine product
influence. Roadmap sessions and design partnerships are the mechanisms through
which their depth and trust translate into real product direction — not just the
illusion of input after decisions are already made.

[Inspiration programs](../programs/1-inspiration.md) amplify Guardians as
storytellers: the practitioner who has done the work, earned the credibility, and
is ready to share it publicly is the most compelling voice in the ecosystem.
