# Donor

**What they give:** Code, knowledge, feedback, advocacy, time.

The Donor contributes to the ecosystem without expecting a direct return on any
specific act of contribution. They answer the question knowing they will not
always be thanked. They write the tutorial because it needed writing. They speak
at the meetup because they remember being in the audience and benefiting from
someone else doing the same thing.

Their motivation is not purely altruistic — reputation matters, craft matters,
the satisfaction of being genuinely useful matters. But they are not keeping a
ledger. There is no transaction here.

## Their role in the graph

Donors are high-outbound nodes: value flows from them. Every contribution adds
an edge between their knowledge and a practitioner who needed it, between their
code and an integration that did not exist before, between their real experience
and the product team that needed to hear it.

No DevRel team, however large, can answer every question, write every tutorial,
or attend every meetup. Donors are the force multipliers. An ecosystem with
active Donors is one where the organisation's reach is extended far beyond what
it could build alone.

## What they look like in practice

- Answers questions in community channels without being asked — including the
  question they themselves asked six months ago
- Writes the guide that explains the thing the documentation does not cover
- Files detailed, reproducible bug reports rather than complaints
- Shows up to the hackathon with something to contribute, not just something
  to build
- Speaks at the meetup about what they built and what they learned, because
  someone else doing the same was the whole reason they showed up the first time

## Why they matter beyond their contributions

Donors carry tacit knowledge the organisation does not have: the workarounds
that are never documented, the real-world architectures nobody wrote up, the
honest assessment of what works and what does not that a salesperson would never
hear.

They are also a leading indicator of ecosystem health. A growing Donor base
means the ecosystem is creating conditions people want to contribute back to.
A shrinking one means something is wrong — in the value exchange, in the
recognition structures, or in the trust that makes contributing feel worthwhile.

## Where they appear in the programs

[Innovation programs](../programs/4-innovation.md) serve Donors directly:
roadmap sessions and design partnerships give the Donor's contribution genuine
consequence — the sense that what they know and say has a visible effect on
what ships.

[Involvement programs](../programs/3-involvement.md) are designed to make
donation feel worthwhile and sustainable: events, open source programs, and
structured recognition that tells Donors their contribution is seen, valued, and
making a difference to the ecosystem they care about.
