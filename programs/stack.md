# STACK

STACK is the operational framework for tending the developer ecosystem graph. It
describes five modes of graph-tending — five distinct ways that DevRel
strengthens the ecosystem, builds the conditions for self-organisation, and
creates the value exchange that sustains both practitioner and organisational
goals.

The five pillars are not program types — they are lenses. Any DevRel program can
be examined through all five. A hackathon is an act of Kinship. It is also
Tutoring when experienced practitioners mentor newcomers, Stewardship when the
hosts are paying attention to who is isolated and who is connecting, Capability
when the outputs feed into the product roadmap, and Autonomy when its outputs
are things practitioners can take away and use independently.

STACK replaced the earlier CATS (Capability, Autonomy, Tutoring, Stewardship)
and CATSS frameworks. The addition of Kinship, and the renaming to STACK,
reflects a more complete understanding of what makes ecosystems resilient. See
[History](../history/README.md) for context.
## Stewardship

**What it is:** The systemic, ongoing practice of surveying and tending the
ecosystem graph as a whole — identifying isolated nodes, maintaining trust
across the network, and ensuring that the conditions for health are continuously
present.

**What it is not:** Individual relationship management. Stewardship operates at
the level of the ecosystem, not the level of any single practitioner. It is the
function of seeing the whole graph: noticing when a segment of the practitioner
population is unrepresented, when a geography is underserved, when an emerging
use case has no community forming around it yet.

Stewardship is custodial in the best sense — the ongoing, often invisible work
of maintaining the conditions that allow everything else to function. It is
least visible when it is working well, and most visible when it has been
neglected: the isolated node who had no idea others were doing the same work,
the community that fragmented because no one was watching the trust levels, the
practitioner population that moved to a competitor because no one noticed they
were drifting.

**Graph mapping:** Stewardship surveys and tends the whole graph. It is the
function responsible for the topology — for knowing what the graph looks like
and for caring about whether it is healthy.
## Tutoring

**What it is:** The deliberate strengthening of individual nodes — the
capability transfer, scaffolding, and confidence-building that moves a
practitioner from where they are to where they need to be.

**What it is not:** Enablement in the product sense, or onboarding in the
customer success sense. Tutoring is grounded in how humans actually learn. It
operates through Bloom's taxonomy: from awareness to understanding, from
understanding to application, from application to analysis, synthesis, and
evaluation. A practitioner who has been through genuine Tutoring does not just
know how to use the technology — they understand it well enough to adapt,
troubleshoot, teach, and extend.

Tutoring is the most cognitively careful pillar. It requires the Tutor to hold
the learner's current mental model, meet them at their genuine starting point,
and build from there — not from the technology's ideal starting point, but from
where the practitioner actually is. This is why the
[Expert Educator](../practitioners/expert-educator.md) is the natural instrument
for Tutoring: the EE's capability is precisely the reduction of cognitive
distance between where someone is and where they need to be.

Tutoring scales poorly in its pure form — one-to-one human teaching is
expensive. DevRel's long-term investment is in building Tutoring infrastructure
that extends beyond individuals: documentation, courses, and community knowledge
that allows practitioners to tutor themselves and each other.

**Graph mapping:** Tutoring strengthens individual nodes — increasing the
capability, confidence, and autonomy of specific practitioners so that each node
carries more weight in the graph.
## Autonomy

**What it is:** The goal state — and the ongoing investment in reaching it —
where practitioners and ecosystem nodes connect and operate independently,
without DevRel as intermediary.

Autonomy is the most honest statement of what DevRel is actually trying to
achieve. The DevRel function that has succeeded is not the one that is most
necessary — it is the one that has made itself least necessary as a central
router. Practitioners who can answer each other's questions, who connect without
introduction, who build things without instruction — they are the evidence that
Autonomy has been built.

This is not the same as irrelevance. The DevRel function at a mature,
self-organising ecosystem is not redundant — it has shifted from tending to
observing, from routing to sensing, from building the connections to maintaining
the conditions. But the individual practitioner no longer depends on DevRel to
navigate the ecosystem.

**What it is not:** Abandonment. Autonomy is not achieved by stepping back — it
is achieved by building the infrastructure, the relationships, and the
distributed knowledge that allows the ecosystem to sustain itself. The
difference between a healthy autonomous ecosystem and an abandoned one is
whether the investment that made self-organisation possible is still present.

**Graph mapping:** Autonomy is the goal — the progressive reduction of DevRel's
role as the necessary intermediary, replaced by direct
practitioner-to-practitioner edges.
## Capability

**What it is:** The collective operational capacity of the ecosystem graph — the
distributed technical competence, the shared patterns, the common tooling and
integration layer that makes the whole ecosystem more capable than the sum of
its individual nodes.

Capability is not just what individual practitioners can do (that is Tutoring).
It is what the ecosystem can do as a collective — the contributions, the
co-created architectures, the integrations that extend the technology's reach,
the reference implementations that become the de facto standard. An ecosystem
with high Capability is one where practitioners have not just learned to use the
technology but have built things that make it more capable for everyone.

Capability is also what flows back to the organisation through the
[Innovation program family](4-innovation.md): the roadmap influence, the design
partnerships, the feature contributions. A capable ecosystem is a co-creator,
not just a consumer.

**What it is not:** A measure of individual skill. Capability in the STACK sense
is about the collective — the ecosystem's aggregate operational competence and
its capacity to extend and improve the technology it depends on.

**Graph mapping:** Capability is the collective load-bearing capacity of the
graph — the ecosystem's ability to produce value, not just consume it.
## Kinship

**What it is:** The quality, density, and resilience of the edges in the graph —
the fellowship under shared technical struggle, the mutual aid, the sense of
belonging to something real.

Kinship is what makes an ecosystem feel like a community rather than a user
base. It is the thing practitioners describe when they say "the people in this
community are genuinely generous" or "I have learned more from the people in
this ecosystem than from any documentation." It is built at hackathons where
strangers become collaborators, in forum threads where the person who answered
the question follows up a week later to see if it helped, at conferences where
the hallway conversation is better than the talks.

Kinship is distinct from networking (which is transactional), from community
management (which is structural and operational), and from advocacy (which is
about external communication). Kinship is relational. It is the quality of the
internal bonds between practitioners — their mutual investment in each other's
success.

Community is structural. Kinship is relational.

The practitioner who stays in an ecosystem despite a better technical
alternative existing elsewhere is usually staying because of Kinship — because
they have relationships, shared history, and mutual obligation with others in
the ecosystem that no product comparison can capture.

**What it is not:** Social activity for its own sake. Kinship without technical
substance is social entertainment. The Kinship that makes an ecosystem resilient
is forged in shared technical struggle — in building things that are hard,
debugging things that are mysterious, and learning things that are genuinely
difficult, alongside people who are doing the same.

**Graph mapping:** Kinship is the quality and density of the edges themselves —
not just whether connections exist, but whether they are genuinely load-bearing,
reciprocal, and durable.
## How STACK relates to the program families

STACK and the four program families are parallel frameworks, not a direct
mapping. STACK describes _modes of graph-tending_. The program families describe
_what DevRel does_ and _which strategic goals it serves_.

Any program can be examined through all five STACK lenses:

| program family | Stewardship | Tutoring | Autonomy | Capability | Kinship |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Information** | Identifies friction that documentation should address | Core delivery mechanism — reduces cognitive load | Builds the self-service layer that reduces dependency | Raises individual and ecosystem capability | Lower — primarily knowledge transfer |
| **Inspiration** | Surveys which stories are being told and which are missing | Contextualises what is worth learning | Generates Attraction that brings new nodes in independently | Amplifies capability that already exists | Moderate — shared inspiration builds fellowship |
| **Involvement** | Actively tends the graph through events and Guardian cultivation | Peer-to-peer learning at events and in OSS | Practitioners routing to each other, not to DevRel | Co-creation and contribution raise collective capability | Highest — direct investment in edge quality |
| **Innovation** | Surfaces signals about ecosystem health back to the organisation | Builds the feedback discipline that improves the product | Gives Guardians genuine product influence | Shapes the technology's capability roadmap | Builds deep Kinship through co-creation |

The framework is not a scorecard — it is a diagnostic. If a program is
delivering on Tutoring but neglecting Kinship, the practitioner gains capability
but may not stay. If it is building Kinship but not Autonomy, it creates loyalty
that is still dependent on DevRel routing. The goal is programs that develop
across all five dimensions over time.
