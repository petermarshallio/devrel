# Inspiration

**Strategic goal:** [Market awareness](../goals/market-awareness.md)
**Journey stages:** [Attraction](../journey/attraction.md) → [Awareness](../journey/awareness.md) → [Advocacy](../journey/advocacy.md)
**Primary practitioner:** [Reputable Resonator](../practitioners/reputable-resonator.md)

---

Inspiration programmes create the conditions for a practitioner to want to pay attention. They do not teach — that is Information's job. They do not build relationships — that is Involvement's job. They make someone feel that a technology, a community, or an idea is worth their time.

This is a different kind of work. It operates at the level of reputation and conviction rather than knowledge or relationship. It is the function that makes practitioners seek something out rather than waiting to be found.

Inspiration programmes serve two moments in the developer journey. The first is early — Attraction and Awareness — when a practitioner has no relationship with the technology yet and is deciding whether to invest attention. The second is late — Advocacy — when a practitioner who has already done the work is ready to share it with others, and needs the platform, the narrative, and sometimes the credibility-by-association to do so effectively.

## Programmes

### Advocacy and content

The practitioner-facing output of the RR instrument: talks at conferences and meetups, written content that reframes how a topic is understood, video explainers built around narrative rather than instruction, and the social presence that makes a technology feel alive and worth following.

| Dimension | Description |
| --- | --- |
| **People** | Led by RR practitioners; EE practitioners contribute depth; partnered with Marketing (distribution, event logistics, brand alignment) and Sales (field event co-ordination) |
| **Process** | Topic selection → content development → review → delivery → distribution → measurement of reach and downstream attribution |
| **Place** | Conferences and third-party events (synchronous, in-person); webinars (synchronous, virtual); blogs and long-form content (asynchronous); social platforms — LinkedIn, X, YouTube (asynchronous) |
| **Technology** | Content management and distribution platforms; video hosting and analytics; social scheduling; AI-assisted drafting and research for first passes |

**Signal:** Content reach; downstream search traffic; attribution of new practitioners to specific content pieces; speaker programme quality (acceptance rate at tier-1 events).

---

### Storytelling

The practitioner success story — the case study, the "powered by" feature, the co-presented talk, the written account of a real production deployment. Storytelling programmes surface the authentic experience of practitioners using the technology at scale.

Storytelling is distinct from marketing case studies. The audience is other practitioners, not buyers. The credibility comes from the practitioner telling the story, not the vendor packaging it. DevRel's role is to identify the stories worth telling, build the relationship that makes the practitioner willing to tell them, and provide the platform and craft support to tell them well.

| Dimension | Description |
| --- | --- |
| **People** | RR practitioners lead the narrative craft; CC practitioners identify and cultivate the relationships that surface the stories; partnered with Marketing (production, distribution) |
| **Process** | Guardian identification → relationship building → story development → production (written, video, or live) → distribution → measurement |
| **Place** | Conference talks (synchronous); blog posts and case study pages (asynchronous); podcast appearances (asynchronous); community spotlight features |
| **Technology** | Content production tooling; distribution platforms; Guardian identification via community analytics tools; AI-assisted interview transcription and story drafting |

**Signal:** Story reach within the target practitioner community; inbound practitioner referrals citing specific stories; Guardian willingness to participate (a proxy for ecosystem trust); downstream adoption attributed to story exposure.

## Practitioner fit

Inspiration is the natural home of the Reputable Resonator. The RR's transferable credibility is what makes a conference talk compelling rather than informational, and what makes a success story feel authentic rather than marketed.

The CC contributes to Inspiration through the Guardian relationships that surface the best stories. The EE contributes through the depth that makes the RR's content unimpeachable — the RR who can say "I built this" or "I spoke to the engineers" is more compelling than the one who cannot.
