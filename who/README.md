# Who does this work?

So you want to work in Developer Relations.

The first thing worth knowing is that DevRel is not a personality type. It is not "technically-minded extrovert who loves people." It is not a specific job title or a career ladder with prescribed rungs. It is a collection of human capabilities, each of which tends the developer ecosystem in a different way.

Most DevRel practitioners carry a blend of these capabilities. But almost everyone has a dominant tendency. Knowing yours — really knowing it, not just preferring it — is what separates people who thrive in DevRel from people who burn out performing a version of it that was never really theirs.

The three fundamental instruments are grounded in something old. Aristotle described three modes of persuasion: logos (reason and evidence), ethos (credibility and trust), and pathos (emotion and conviction). DevRel practitioners, it turns out, are still working in exactly those modes — just applied to developer ecosystems rather than Athenian courts.

---

## The Expert Educator — Logos

The Expert Educator's instrument is knowledge and evidence.

The EE moves people up through Bloom's taxonomy: from awareness, through understanding, to application, analysis, synthesis, and evaluation. They make complex things accessible. They know not just what the technology does but how to transfer that understanding to someone who doesn't yet have it — and how to scaffold that transfer so the learner arrives with confidence, not confusion.

Knowledge flows outward. The EE's stock-in-trade is deep technical or conceptual expertise, deployed in service of other people's capability.

The archetypal EE is David Attenborough. Not because he is a naturalist (though he is), but because he takes something genuinely complex and makes it feel graspable — without dumbing it down, without losing the wonder, without pretending it is simpler than it is. The knowledge is real. The communication is a craft.

EE is often expressed on stage or in workshops. But the introvert writing the definitive reference guide is equally an EE — the mode of expression varies, the instrument is the same. An EE can fill a room or fill a documentation site.

Practitioners often arrive at EE through engineering, pre-sales engineering, solutions architecture, or curriculum design. The technical knowledge is already there. What they develop in DevRel is the communication craft — the ability to meet a practitioner exactly where they are and move them somewhere more capable.

**What development looks like for an EE:** The knowledge is already there. What an EE develops is the accessibility — the craft of translation. How do you explain this without losing the person? How do you know when someone has understood, not just heard? How do you write documentation that people actually read?

## The Connected Correspondent — Ethos

The Connected Correspondent's instrument is trust and credibility.

The CC builds relationships — not transactional ones, not strategic ones, but relationships so genuine and non-instrumental that people say things they would never say to a salesperson or a marketer. The CC is the person who gets the unfiltered story. The real product feedback. The honest opinion that nobody posts in the public forum.

Truth flows inward. Where the EE sends knowledge out, the CC brings truth back. That intelligence — about what practitioners actually struggle with, what they trust, what they tell each other — is some of the most valuable input any DevRel function can offer.

The philosophical grounding here is Michael Polanyi's concept of tacit knowledge: "We know more than we can tell." Practitioners hold enormous amounts of knowledge they cannot easily articulate — intuitions, workarounds, hard-won lessons. The CC creates the conditions in which that knowledge surfaces.

The archetypal CC is Louis Theroux. Not because he is a journalist (though he is), but because he has the remarkable ability to make people forget they are being heard. He is genuinely curious, genuinely non-judgemental, and genuinely interested — and people open up to him in ways they do not open up to others. That is the CC gift.

The CC can be a networker who thrives in a room full of new people. But they can equally be someone who builds extraordinarily deep one-on-one relationships over years. The depth of the connection matters more than the breadth.

Practitioners often arrive at CC through marketing, events management, journalism, or community management. What they unlearn in DevRel is the broadcast instinct — the habit of always having a message to deliver. The CC's development challenge is the courage of genuinely no-agenda relationships.

**What development looks like for a CC:** The relationships are already there. What a CC develops is the discipline of non-transactional presence — showing up without an agenda, without a quota, without a message to land. This is harder than it sounds. It requires unlearning almost everything that marketing and sales instil about purposeful communication.

## The Reputable Resonator — Pathos

The Reputable Resonator's instrument is conviction.

The RR does not merely inform (that is the EE) or extract (that is the CC). The RR brings people to conviction. They move audiences — in rooms, in articles, in introductions — through the accumulated weight of story, experience, craft, and credibility. Their reputation is transferable: attaching their name or presence to something elevates it.

The philosophical grounding here is John Henry Newman's concept of the illative sense — the mind's capacity to reason toward certainty through cumulative evidence, none of which is decisive alone. Newman wrote: "The mind ranges to and fro, and ventures and commits itself, and now is successful in its issue, and now is baffled... It decides on cumulative evidence." The RR creates those cumulative conditions. They are not persuading by logic alone or by charm alone, but by the total weight of their credibility applied over time.

The reference points here are Nancy Duarte's subjects — people who change how an audience sees the world — and practitioners like Tim Berglund and Charity Majors, whose public credibility is so established that their endorsement or presence is itself meaningful.

Critically, the RR skill is distinct. It is not derived from EE or CC. You cannot become an RR simply by knowing a lot or knowing a lot of people. The craft of translation and resonance — of taking something true and making it felt — is its own capability.

But it requires grounding. Resonance without substance is hollow. Conviction without knowledge is manipulation. Conviction without trust is propaganda. The RR who has not done the EE work — who does not actually understand the technical domain deeply — is performing resonance, not delivering it. And practitioners will notice.

**What development looks like for an RR:** The craft of resonance is already there. What an RR develops is substantive depth — enough technical or relational grounding that the resonance is unimpeachable, not just charismatic. This often means investing deliberately in EE or CC development, not as a replacement for RR but as the foundation that makes it durable.

## Luminary

Luminary is not a fourth archetype. It is a career stage.

A Luminary is an Expert Educator, Connected Correspondent, or Reputable Resonator whose reputation now precedes them. They are sought, not seeking. Programme committees compete for them. Practitioners look forward to seeing them speak, reading what they write, or simply being in the room with them.

Luminary status is earned — through consistency, through genuine contribution, through the long accumulation of credibility. It cannot be manufactured by a PR strategy. It can be cultivated by doing the work well, over time, without shortcuts.

In some markets — particularly in ASEAN, where earned credibility is culturally non-negotiable — Luminary-level practitioners are not merely valuable. They are the condition under which the ecosystem trusts the company at all.

---

## A note on personality

These archetypes do not map to personality types.

An Expert Educator can be the most extroverted person in the room or a deeply introverted writer who hates public speaking. A Connected Correspondent can be a natural networker or someone who builds a small number of extremely deep relationships over years. A Reputable Resonator can own a stage or write the article everyone cannot wait to read.

The instrument is not the performance. It is the underlying capability — the mode in which you generate value, the direction in which your particular gift flows.

Most DevRel practitioners are a blend. Growth does not mean becoming a different archetype. It means developing the edges of the one (or ones) that are genuinely yours.
