# Reputable Resonator

**Aristotelian grounding:** Pathos — the instrument of conviction, resonance, and the illative sense.

**Real-world exemplars:** Nancy Duarte's subjects; Tim Berglund; Charity Majors. People whose public credibility is so established that their endorsement or presence is itself meaningful. People who change how an audience sees the world.

**Distinguishing characteristic:** The RR brings people to conviction — not through information alone (that is the EE) or through trust alone (that is the CC) but through the accumulated weight of story, experience, craft, and credibility. The philosophical grounding is Newman's illative sense: "The mind ranges to and fro... gaining one by one turns of evidence... deciding on cumulative evidence." The RR creates those cumulative conditions. Their reputation is transferable.

Critically: resonance without substance is hollow. Conviction without knowledge is manipulation. Conviction without trust is propaganda. The RR capability is distinct — but it requires grounding in EE or CC work to be durable.
