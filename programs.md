# Programs

DevRel delivers its work through programs — structured activities organised
around a clear purpose, a target audience at a particular stage of their
journey, and a measurable outcome.

Those programs fall into four families. Each family serves a different strategic
goal. Each sits alongside one or more stages of the developer journey. And each
can be described using the same four-dimension framework that applies to any
organisational service: **People, Process, Place, and Technology**.

The operational framework behind all of this is [STACK](programs/stack.md) —
Stewardship, Tutoring, Autonomy, Capability, Kinship. STACK describes the five
modes of graph-tending that any DevRel program can be examined through. The four
program families describe _what_ DevRel does; STACK describes _how_ it tends the
ecosystem.
## The developer journey

The journey a practitioner takes from first encounter to active contributor
follows seven stages:

| Stage | The practitioner's question |
| --- | --- |
| **Attraction** | Is there something here worth paying attention to? |
| **Awareness** | What is this, and is it relevant to me? |
| **Assessment** | Should I use this? Can I? |
| **Adoption** | I am building with this. |
| **Acclimation** | I am running this in production. I am finding my feet. |
| **Application** | I am getting real value from this at scale. |
| **Advocacy** | I want others to know about this. I want to contribute. |

Acclimation is the stage most often overlooked. A practitioner who has just gone
to production is not yet an advocate — they are stabilising. They are hitting
the real edges, forming real opinions, and deciding whether this technology is
worth their long-term commitment. The practitioner who is well-supported through
Acclimation is significantly more likely to reach Advocacy. The one who is not
often silently churns.

The developer journey also maps onto the software development lifecycle. The
pre-production phases — Plan, Code, Build, Test — correspond to Attraction
through Adoption, where the
[Expert Educator](../practitioners/expert-educator.md) is most naturally at
home. The post-production phases — Release, Deploy, Operate, Monitor —
correspond to Acclimation through Advocacy, where the
[Connected Correspondent](../practitioners/connected-correspondent.md) is most
effective. The [Reputable Resonator](../practitioners/reputable-resonator.md) is
valuable at both ends: inspiring entry at Attraction and Awareness, and
amplifying Guardians back into the ecosystem at Advocacy.
## The four program families

| Family | Strategic goal | Journey stages |
| --- | --- | --- |
| [Inspiration](programs/1-inspiration.md) | Market awareness | Attraction → Awareness → Advocacy |
| [Information](programs/2-information.md) | Adoption | Assessment → Adoption → Acclimation |
| [Involvement](programs/3-involvement.md) | Loyalty | Acclimation → Application → Advocacy |
| [Innovation](programs/4-innovation.md) | Product development | Application → Advocacy |

program scope and priority depend on the organisation's emphasis on paid versus
unpaid relationships. An open-source-led organisation will weight Involvement
and Information differently than a commercial SaaS organisation.
## How each program is described

Each program in this section is described across four dimensions. This follows
the standard service description pattern used across IT service management and
enterprise architecture:

**People** — which practitioner type(s) lead the program, and which internal
partners are involved (Engineering, Marketing, Customer Success, Product, Sales)

**Process** — the workflow, campaign structure, or program design: how it is
initiated, run, and closed

**Place** — the channels through which it is delivered, both physical (events,
offices, conferences) and logical (forums, Slack, documentation sites, video
platforms). Channels vary in two important ways:

- _Synchronous_ channels exist at a specific time and place. They allow
  immediate interaction and relationship-making, but are not personalised and
  may not meet varying competencies.
- _Asynchronous_ channels are accessed on demand. They support self-paced
  consumption and scale broadly, but require practitioners to already be aware
  of and motivated to find them.

**Technology** — the tooling, platforms, and automation that support or could
replace human effort in the program's delivery

## Intel

Intel is not a fifth program family — it is the observability layer that runs
beneath all four.

DevRel's unique contribution to the business is knowledge of the ecosystem that
no other function holds. Intel is the systematic practice of surfacing that
knowledge: identifying emerging Guardians, tracking ecosystem health signals,
detecting friction that does not appear in support tickets, understanding
adoption patterns before they show up in usage data.

Every program generates Intel signals as a by-product. The organisation that
instruments those signals — and routes them to the teams that need them
(Product, Sales, Customer Success, Leadership) — has a structural advantage over
the one that does not.

Signal types to track:

- **Ecosystem health** — ratio of questions to answers in community channels;
  contributor diversity; time to first meaningful response
- **Guardian emergence** — practitioners who are answering more than asking; who
  are being cited by others; who are contributing back
- **Adoption friction** — the questions that recur; the concepts that reliably
  confuse; the stages where practitioners stall
- **Dependency patterns** — which integrations, use cases, and architectures are
  practitioners actually building
