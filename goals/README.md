# Strategic goals

DevRel's four programme families each serve a distinct organisational goal. These goals describe what the organisation is trying to achieve through its investment in the ecosystem — the outcomes that justify the programmes.

| Goal | Programme family | Primary journey stages |
|---|---|---|
| [Adoption](adoption.md) | [Information](../programs/information.md) | Assessment → Adoption → Acclimation |
| [Market awareness](market-awareness.md) | [Inspiration](../programs/inspiration.md) | Attraction → Awareness → Advocacy |
| [Loyalty](loyalty.md) | [Involvement](../programs/involvement.md) | Acclimation → Application → Advocacy |
| [Product development](product-development.md) | [Innovation](../programs/innovation.md) | Application → Advocacy |

The goals are distinct but not independent. Adoption depends on Market awareness creating the conditions for practitioners to discover the technology. Loyalty depends on Adoption having delivered real value. Product development depends on Loyalty having built the trust that enables honest feedback. The four goals form a sequence as well as a portfolio.

Programme scope and priority depend on the organisation's context: its stage of market development, its balance of paid versus unpaid relationships, and where the ecosystem currently sits on the [maturity model](../maturity/README.md).
