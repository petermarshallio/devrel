# Market awareness (strategic goal)

**program family:** [Inspiration](../programs/1-inspiration.md) **Driven by:**
Advocacy and content, Storytelling

Market awareness is the organisational goal of ensuring that the right
practitioners, in the right contexts, at the right moments, are aware that this
technology exists and is worth their attention. It is not about maximising reach
— it is about ensuring that the practitioners for whom this technology is
genuinely relevant know about it and have formed an accurate initial impression
of it.

This is the goal that the
[Inspiration program family](../programs/1-inspiration.md) is primarily designed
to serve. Everything that creates genuine [Attraction](../journey/attraction.md)
and [Awareness](../journey/awareness.md) in the target practitioner population
is a Market awareness investment.
## What market awareness actually means

Market awareness is frequently conflated with marketing reach — the number of
impressions, followers, downloads, or event attendees. These are measures of
exposure, not of awareness. Exposure and awareness are not the same thing.

**Exposure** is seeing something. It is passive and requires no engagement from
the practitioner.

**Awareness** is having formed a mental model. It is the practitioner who, when
the technology is mentioned in a conversation, can say with confidence: I know
what that is, I know what it does, and I know whether it is relevant to me. That
practitioner has Awareness. The one who has seen the brand name on a banner but
cannot describe the technology has been exposed, not made Aware.

The distinction matters because the Inspiration investments that produce genuine
Awareness are different from the ones that produce exposure. A conference
sponsorship with a logo on a banner produces exposure. A practitioner giving an
honest, technically substantive talk about how they used the technology to solve
a real problem produces Awareness — in the audience members for whom the problem
is relevant.

DevRel's goal is practitioner-relevant awareness: the right practitioners have a
functional mental model of the technology that helps them make an informed
Assessment decision. The goal is not to maximise the number of people who have
seen the brand — it is to ensure that the practitioners who should be
considering the technology know about it and understand it accurately.
## The relationship between market awareness and the practitioner journey

Market awareness maps to the earliest stages of the
[practitioner journey](../programs.md): [Attraction](../journey/attraction.md)
(the signal that makes a practitioner notice) and
[Awareness](../journey/awareness.md) (the understanding they form when they
investigate).

It also maps to the final stage — [Advocacy](../journey/advocacy.md) — in a
different way. The practitioners who have completed the full journey and are now
Advocates are the primary generators of market awareness for the next generation
of practitioners. The Guardian who speaks at a conference, writes the blog post,
or recommends the technology to a colleague is doing more for Market awareness
than any amount of vendor-generated content, because the signal comes from a
peer rather than a promoter.

This creates a flywheel: Market awareness generates Adoption → Adoption
generates Application → Application generates Guardians → Guardians generate
Market awareness. DevRel's role is to keep the flywheel turning by investing
simultaneously in both ends: creating the initial Attraction and Awareness that
seeds the journey, and cultivating the Guardians and Advocates who sustain it at
scale.
## What the organisation needs to do

**Invest in practitioner voices, not just vendor voices.** The most credible
Market awareness content comes from practitioners speaking from genuine
experience. The organisation's own content has a ceiling of credibility —
practitioners know it is vendor-produced and discount it accordingly.
Practitioner-produced content — talks, articles, case studies, and the quiet
recommendation one engineer makes to another — reaches the target audience with
a level of trust that vendor content cannot manufacture.

This means DevRel must invest in the Guardian and Advocacy infrastructure that
makes practitioner content possible: identifying practitioners with relevant
experience and something worth saying, supporting them in developing and sharing
it, and creating platforms and distribution channels that make their content
visible.

**Be specific about who the target practitioner is.** Market awareness as a goal
without audience specificity is difficult to pursue meaningfully. The
practitioner population for any given technology has characteristics: the kinds
of problems they work on, the technology stacks they operate in, the communities
where they gather, the voices they trust. Market awareness investment is most
efficient when it is targeted — reaching the practitioners for whom the
technology is genuinely relevant rather than broadcasting to the widest possible
audience.

**Ensure Attraction leads to accurate Awareness.** The Market awareness goal is
not just served by exposure — it requires that the mental model practitioners
form is accurate. A practitioner who develops Awareness based on overclaimed
marketing content will arrive at Assessment with expectations that reality
cannot meet. The short-term appearance of Market awareness success (many
practitioners investigated) can mask a downstream failure (none of them adopted,
because the real product did not match the marketing model).

**Measure practitioner-level outcomes.** Market awareness is often measured in
impressions, video views, and event attendance. These are proxies for Exposure.
A more useful set of metrics tracks practitioner-level outcomes: the
Assessment-stage practitioner who can describe the technology accurately, the
pipeline of practitioners moving from Attraction into Awareness and from
Awareness into Assessment. The quality of Market awareness is better assessed by
what happens after exposure than by the exposure itself.
## The tension with other organisational goals

Market awareness is the goal that most naturally invites the conflation of
DevRel with marketing. Marketing functions are excellent at reaching large
audiences. DevRel is excellent at reaching the right practitioners with the
right credibility. These are different skills operating at different scales.

The tension arises when Market awareness investments are measured by marketing
metrics (reach, share of voice, impression volume) rather than
practitioner-relevant outcomes (assessment conversion, quality of practitioner
mental models). DevRel measured against marketing metrics will eventually become
marketing — optimising for reach at the expense of credibility, publishing
content that generates impressions but does not move practitioners through the
journey.

The organisation that wants DevRel to serve the Market awareness goal needs to
give it the right brief: not "reach as many practitioners as possible" but
"ensure that the practitioners who should be considering this technology have an
accurate picture of it."
## DevRel's role

The primary practitioner instrument for Market awareness is the
[Reputable Resonator](../practitioners/reputable-resonator.md). The RR's
transferable credibility — the capacity to make an audience feel that something
is worth their time — is the capability that most directly drives genuine
Awareness rather than mere exposure. The RR's conference talk, the piece of
content they write or endorse, the introduction they make on behalf of a
Guardian who has something worth saying — all of these create the kind of
Attraction and Awareness that moves practitioners toward Assessment.

The [Connected Correspondent](../practitioners/connected-correspondent.md)
contributes through the Guardian relationships that surface the practitioner
voices most credible to the target audience.

The [Inspiration program family](../programs/1-inspiration.md) is the structural
home of the Market awareness goal: Advocacy and content, Storytelling,
conference presence, and the social visibility that makes a technology feel
alive and worth following.
