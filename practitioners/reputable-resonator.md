# Reputable Resonator

**Instrument:** Pathos — conviction, via the accumulation of evidence and
credibility **Archetype:** Tim Berglund, Charity Majors **Craft reference:**
Nancy Duarte
## What they do

The Reputable Resonator does not inform and does not extract — they bring people
to conviction.

Cardinal Newman described a particular mode of reasoning he called the illative
sense: "The mind ranges to and fro, and bathes itself in the accumulation of
evidence... gaining one by one turns of evidence... and deciding on cumulative
evidence." The RR works by this logic. They do not make a single argument. They
accumulate — story, experience, craft, presence, credibility — until the weight
of it becomes compelling. The listener does not just understand; they believe.

This is a distinct capability. It is not a more intense version of the EE's
knowledge transfer, and it is not the CC's relationship depth. It is the ability
to translate both into something that moves people — that changes their sense of
what is possible, what is worth doing, what they want to be part of.

The RR's reputation is transferable. When they attach their name or presence to
something, it is elevated. program committees compete for them. Organisations
invite them not because of what they will say specifically, but because of what
their presence signals — that this is worth your time, that this technology is
serious, that this community is real.
## What they look like in practice

The RR is often the most visible DevRel practitioner from the outside. They are
the person whose conference talk people quote for years. Whose blog post changed
how a field thought about a problem. Whose introduction at an event makes the
speaker seem immediately worth listening to.

But visibility is a symptom, not the capability. The RR is compelling in small
rooms as well as large ones. They can write the piece that 50,000 people share
or have the conversation that changes one person's career trajectory. The scale
of the audience is not what defines them — the quality of the conviction they
produce is.

They are also often the people who make other people look good. The endorsement,
the introduction, the foreword — these are RR acts. The credibility flows
sideways as well as forward.
## The critical caveat

Resonance without substance is hollow. Conviction without knowledge is
manipulation. Conviction without trust is propaganda.

The RR who is only charismatic — who can move audiences without the grounding of
genuine expertise (EE) or genuine relationships (CC) — is a short-term asset and
a long-term liability. Practitioners are not audiences; they are technically
sophisticated peers who will eventually notice if the depth is not there. When
it is not, the credibility collapses, and it is very hard to rebuild.

The RR's instrument requires grounding in at least one of the other two.
Technical depth serves the resonance. Relationship depth gives it texture and
honesty. Without either, what the RR has is performance — which is different,
and much less durable.
## Where they come from

RRs most often arrive in DevRel from engineering, writing, public speaking, or
roles where communication was itself the product — journalism, teaching, product
marketing done well. They may not always have recognised their instrument until
they were already using it.

They are often people who have thought carefully about craft: how to structure a
narrative, how to land a point, how to make something abstract feel immediate.
They read widely and across domains. They have a collector's instinct for the
analogy that will make something click.
## Where they thrive in the developer journey

The RR is valuable across the full developer journey, but is most impactful at
the transitions: the moment when a practitioner first encounters a technology
and needs a reason to invest attention (Attraction, Awareness), and the moment
when a practitioner is ready to become a Guardian and needs the conviction to
start giving back (Advocacy).

In both cases, information alone is not what moves people. The EE can tell them
what to learn. The RR can make them want to.
## What they need to develop

The RR's development challenge is almost always substantive depth — not
communication craft, which is usually already present.

Specifically:

- **Technical grounding** — enough expertise that the resonance is
  unimpeachable. The RR does not need to be the deepest technical person in the
  room, but they need to be credible to the deepest technical person in the
  room.
- **Relationship depth** — enough genuine connection to the ecosystem that the
  stories they tell are real, not constructed. The RR who recycles the same
  three anecdotes eventually runs out of material and credibility
  simultaneously.
- **Humility about limits** — knowing which topics they can speak to with
  authority and which they cannot. The RR who speaks confidently beyond their
  depth is the most dangerous version of the hollow resonator.
