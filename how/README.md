# How DevRel is built and run

If [why](../why/README.md) is the theory and [who](../who/README.md) is the instrument, this is the practice.

DevRel tends the developer ecosystem in systematic ways. The operational framework for that tending is STACK.

---

## STACK

STACK describes the five modes through which DevRel shapes the graph.

**Stewardship** is surveying and tending the whole graph. Identifying isolated nodes. Outreach to practitioners who have gone quiet. Ongoing check-in. Trust maintenance. Custodial care of the ecosystem as a system — not just serving individuals who show up, but actively looking for those who haven't. Stewardship is systemic; it is not the same as mentoring, which is a relationship with a specific person. A Steward tends the garden. A mentor tends a plant.

**Tutoring** is strengthening individual nodes. It is Bloom's taxonomy in practice — reducing cognitive load, scaffolding understanding, building capability and confidence in specific practitioners. The human learning mechanism: meeting someone where they are and moving them somewhere more capable. Tutoring is not the same as building self-service resources — that is Autonomy. Tutoring involves a human in the loop — the practitioner who knows when someone has understood, not just heard.

**Autonomy** is the goal state where practitioners connect and operate independently, without DevRel as an intermediary. Self-sufficiency. Reduced dependency on the vendor to route every question, answer every query, translate every concept. The documentation that answers the question at 2am. The community thread where another practitioner has already solved the problem. Autonomy is the measure of ecosystem health: how well can practitioners help themselves and each other without DevRel in the room?

**Capability** is the collective operational capacity of the graph. Feature development that the ecosystem requested. Roadmap influence. Ecosystem competence — not just individual skill, but the distributed ability of the ecosystem to build, operate, and extend the technology. Capability is what makes the graph load-bearing: the ability of the ecosystem, not just the vendor, to carry the work.

**Kinship** is the quality, density, and resilience of the edges. Fellowship under shared technical struggle. Hackathons. Open source collaboration. Conferences and gatherings where practitioners meet as peers. Mutual aid. Collective resilience. Kinship is not networking — networking is transactional. Kinship is not community management — community is structural, kinship is relational. Kinship is what makes practitioners care about the ecosystem beyond their immediate self-interest.

> "Community is structural. Kinship is relational."

## Mapping STACK to the graph

Each STACK pillar operates on a different part of the graph:

| Pillar | Graph operation |
|---|---|
| Stewardship | Surveying and tending the whole graph |
| Tutoring | Strengthening individual nodes |
| Autonomy | Enabling nodes to operate without DevRel as intermediary |
| Capability | Building the collective load-bearing capacity of the graph |
| Kinship | Increasing the quality and density of the edges |

## The four programme families

DevRel activity clusters into four families. Each family inputs to a different strategic goal:

**Information** drives Adoption. This is the knowledge and education work: documentation, tutorials, notebooks, courses, reference guides, interactive labs. The goal is reducing the friction of technical adoption — making it easier to understand, easier to try, easier to build.

**Inspiration** drives Market awareness. This is the evangelism and storytelling work: conference talks, blog posts, demos, case studies, content that makes practitioners aware something exists and curious enough to investigate it. Inspiration is the work that moves people from not knowing to wanting to know more.

**Involvement** drives Loyalty. This is the open source contribution, hackathon, event, and co-creation work — the programmes that invite practitioners to participate in the ecosystem, not just consume from it. Involvement deepens the edges. It turns Beneficiaries into Donors.

**Innovation** drives Product development. This is the feedback, roadmap influence, and feature contribution work — the programmes that bring practitioner knowledge into the product itself. Innovation is how the ecosystem shapes what gets built.

Programme scope and priority depend on the organisation's emphasis on paid versus unpaid relationships. A product-led growth company will weigh Information and Innovation heavily. A brand-led company may weight Inspiration. The mix is always a strategic choice.

## Intel

Intel is not a fifth programme family. It is the observability layer that runs beneath all four.

Every programme — every talk, every clinic, every hackathon, every forum thread — generates signals. Intel is the discipline of capturing, synthesising, and routing those signals to the people in the organisation who need them.

Intel produces:

- **Ecosystem health signals** — is the graph becoming more connected or more isolated? Are Guardians emerging or atrophying?
- **Friction signals** — where are practitioners getting stuck? What questions keep recurring? What does the support queue reveal about the documentation?
- **Dependency signals** — which nodes are load-bearing? What would happen if they left?
- **Emerging Guardian signals** — which practitioners are starting to give back? Who should be nurtured?

Intel is DevRel's most strategically valuable contribution to the wider business. No other function is in the position to surface this knowledge — because no other function has the non-transactional relationships required to hear it honestly.

## Cross-functional alignment

DevRel is the hub between primary and support functions. Not a controller. A facilitator.

Each programme family maps to the functions it primarily interfaces with:

| Programme family | Primary interfaces |
|---|---|
| Information | Product, Docs, Customer Success |
| Inspiration | Marketing, Sales |
| Involvement | Engineering, Open Source, Events |
| Innovation | Product, R&D |
| Intel | Sales, Product, Customer Success, Leadership |

The goal of cross-functional alignment is not for DevRel to own the relationship with every function. It is to ensure that the knowledge DevRel holds — about the graph, about practitioners, about where friction lives and where trust is forming — reaches the people who can act on it.

DevRel opens doors. Brings people together. Reduces organisational friction. The work that happens after is everyone's.
