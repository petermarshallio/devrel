# The Altruist

The Altruist contributes because contributing is intrinsically satisfying. They are not building a profile, chasing recognition, or working toward a business outcome. They want to make the technology better and to be part of the work of making it better.

Altruists are often deeply skilled — not always the loudest voice, but frequently among the most technically capable contributors in the ecosystem. They care about craft: clean code, well-structured documentation, problems solved properly rather than quickly.

## What drives them

The Altruist is motivated by the work itself. They want to build things that matter, solve problems that are genuinely hard, and contribute to something larger than their day job. They are drawn to open source not as a career move but as an expression of values — code as a public good, knowledge as something that should circulate freely.

They are often more interested in the technical dimension of the ecosystem than the social one. The pull request is more natural to them than the conference talk.

## Where they get stuck

Altruists can be frustrated by contribution friction — processes that are slow, unclear, or unwelcoming. They can also disengage when their work is not acknowledged or when the direction of the project diverges from what they care about technically.

They are not trying to win an argument. They are trying to do good work. When the environment makes that hard, they quietly withdraw.

## What moves them forward

- Clear contribution pathways and responsive maintainers
- Technical depth — documentation and content that respects their expertise
- A project committee they respect and can contribute to as a peer
- Recognition that is specific to the work, not generic praise

## In the ecosystem

The Altruist is a high-value [Donor](donor.md). Their contributions — code, documentation, issue triage, reviews — are often invisible until they stop. DevRel's role is to reduce the friction of contribution, to create conditions in which Altruists can do the work they came to do.

Altruists are natural Guardian candidates. Their investment deepens their attachment to the ecosystem. They are worth identifying early.
