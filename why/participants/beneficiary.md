# Beneficiary

**What they receive:** Capability, confidence, solutions, shortcuts. Beneficiaries receive value from the ecosystem — the documentation that unblocks them, the forum thread that already contains the answer, the talk that clarifies a concept they'd been carrying unclearly for months.

**Role in the graph:** Beneficiaries are inward-flowing nodes. They are the practitioners who find what they need, use it well, and get on with their work. Every healthy ecosystem has a large proportion of Beneficiaries — and that is not a problem. Beneficiaries are the measure of the ecosystem's reach. An ecosystem that cannot serve Beneficiaries well is one that has lost sight of its purpose.

**Example behaviours:**
- Using documentation, tutorials, and reference materials to build with the technology
- Attending talks and workshops to develop understanding
- Asking questions in forums, Slack, and community spaces
- Adopting patterns and practices they encounter in the ecosystem
- Building things — products, proofs of concept, experiments — that demonstrate adoption

---

## DevRel's role

DevRel's primary obligation to Beneficiaries is to reduce friction. The Beneficiary asking a question at 2am needs an answer — not a relationship. The practitioner trying to understand a concept needs clear explanation — not an invitation to a webinar.

Beneficiaries are also Donors and Guardians in waiting. DevRel watches for the Beneficiary who starts answering questions, the one who files a detailed bug report instead of just abandoning the project, the one who shows up to the hackathon. Those signals matter. The transition from Beneficiary to Donor — and eventually to Guardian — is one of the most important movements in ecosystem development.
