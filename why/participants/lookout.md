# The Lookout

The Lookout is the innovation scout. They sit in innovation teams, architectural functions, or technical strategy roles, scanning the horizon for technologies that might meet known or imagined business needs. They are not here to implement — they are here to find and signal. Their job is to get enough information to let the people back home decide whether it is worth sending a [Knight](knight.md) to look more closely.

Lookouts are often the first people in an organisation to hear about a technology. They move fast across many topics rather than deeply into any one.

## What drives them

The Lookout wants to be the person who found it, made the case for it, and won. Discovery and recognition are intertwined motivations. They are genuinely curious — intellectually stimulated by novelty, drawn to the frontier of what is technically possible.

They care less about implementation depth than about conceptual reach. The big idea matters more than the execution details.

## Where they get stuck

The Lookout needs fast answers to sharp questions:

- Is this technology at the frontier, or already established?
- Does it solve a business problem I care about?
- Is the excitement around it real, or hype?

Content that assumes prior knowledge, buries the lead, or front-loads implementation detail will lose them. They need the clearest possible signal of what the technology is and why it matters — quickly enough that they can brief the people who will decide whether to investigate further.

## What moves them forward

- Compelling, high-level narrative about what the technology makes possible
- Clear positioning relative to alternatives they already know
- Forward-looking content about where the technology is headed
- Use cases that translate technical capability into business relevance

## In the ecosystem

The Lookout is an early-stage [Beneficiary](beneficiary.md) — not yet committed, still assessing. Their value to the ecosystem is upstream: they are the practitioners who bring new technologies to the attention of their organisations. If the Lookout's signal is strong, a Knight arrives. If the Knight's assessment is positive, adoption begins.

DevRel's job with Lookouts is to make the signal as clear as possible, as early as possible. A good piece of Inspiration content is the Lookout's entry point into the ecosystem.
