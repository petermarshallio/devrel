# Donor

**What they give:** Code, knowledge, feedback, advocacy, time. Donors contribute to the ecosystem — more than they take in the short term. They write the blog post, answer the forum question, speak at the conference, file the well-articulated bug report.

**Role in the graph:** Donors are outward-flowing nodes. They create edges by pushing knowledge, content, and capability into the ecosystem. Without Donors, the graph has no circulation. Every practitioner who has ever benefited from a blog post, an answered Stack Overflow question, or an open source library has benefited from a Donor.

**Example behaviours:**
- Writing technical content that helps others solve problems they haven't solved yet
- Answering questions in public forums — not for credit, but because they know the answer
- Contributing to open source projects, including documentation and issue triage
- Giving talks at conferences and meetups
- Mentoring newer practitioners

---

## DevRel's role

DevRel creates conditions for Donors to emerge and remain active. This means: reducing the friction of contribution (making it easy to write, publish, speak, contribute), recognising Donors without instrumentalising them, and understanding what motivates their generosity so that recognition aligns with it.

Not all Donors are visible. Some of the most valuable Donor behaviour is quiet — the practitioner who consistently helps in private Slack channels, the one who reviews PRs without being asked. DevRel's Intel function surfaces these people before they become recognised as the Guardians they are becoming.
