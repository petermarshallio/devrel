# The Tutor

The Tutor shares knowledge because they find it genuinely satisfying to watch someone understand something they did not understand before. They are not seeking a stage or building a following. They show up in office hours, drop-in sessions, forums, and direct messages — the high-touch, one-to-one settings where learning happens person to person.

The Tutor is distinct from the [Mechanic](mechanic.md). The Mechanic's gift is depth of expertise. The Tutor's gift is the ability and willingness to transmit knowledge in the moment, without an audience, without formal structure, in response to wherever the other person actually is.

## What drives them

The Tutor is motivated by the moment of understanding — the point at which a practitioner who was stuck becomes unstuck. They are patient, generous with their time, and genuinely interested in the people they help rather than in the problems abstractly.

They tend not to be interested in the spotlight. The 1:1 conversation is their natural environment. They do not need credit to keep showing up.

## Where they get stuck

Tutors can be undervalued precisely because their work is invisible. They do not produce content that can be indexed. Their impact does not show up in dashboards. If the ecosystem does not create visible pathways for their kind of contribution, they remain hidden — known only to the practitioners they have helped directly.

They can also become fatigued if demand is high and there is no mechanism for the knowledge they share to become durable — if they are answering the same question repeatedly without any system to capture and redistribute what they know.

## What moves them forward

- Structured but informal access points: office hours, drop-ins, mentorship programmes
- Recognition that is personal and specific — not metrics-based
- Pathways to convert what they know into lasting artefacts if they choose to
- A community of peers who share the same instinct toward generosity

## In the ecosystem

The Tutor is a quietly load-bearing [Donor](donor.md). Their contributions are relational rather than artefactual — they leave marks in the confidence and capability of practitioners rather than in repositories or documentation. DevRel's Intel function should watch for them: the practitioner who is consistently helpful in forums, in private threads, in the spaces where nobody is keeping score. These are often the Guardians-in-waiting who are least visible and most at risk of going unrecognised.
