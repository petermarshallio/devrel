# The Mechanic

The Mechanic is a deep technical expert. They operate at tier 3 or tier 4 support depth — the person called when nobody else has been able to diagnose the problem. They are not generalists. They know their domain at a level most practitioners never reach, and they are known for it. People inside and outside their organisation know their name when a hard problem needs solving.

## What drives them

The Mechanic cares that things work correctly and that people use them correctly. They are not satisfied with "good enough" — they want the most out of a system and are willing to say so when they see it being used poorly.

Their ambition is mastery. They want to be the best in their field, and they measure themselves against that standard. Recognition comes from being the person others turn to — not from a job title, but from a reputation earned through depth of knowledge.

## Where they get stuck

The Mechanic's challenge is a high bar for content. Surface-level documentation does not serve them. They need to understand not just what a system does but why it does it — the design decisions, the tradeoffs, the failure modes, the edge cases. Generic getting-started material is something they have already consumed and moved past.

The Mechanic also needs challenges that are worthy of their ability. If the ecosystem does not provide enough depth, they find their own.

## What moves them forward

- Deep technical documentation — reference material, internals, architecture decisions
- Direct access to engineering teams for genuinely hard problems
- Advanced content: deep-dive talks, under-the-covers videos, architecture explainers
- Peer recognition from other Mechanics and from the engineering teams building the technology

## In the ecosystem

The Mechanic is a high-value [Guardian](guardian.md). Their influence extends well beyond their own organisation. When they endorse an approach or flag a problem, the ecosystem listens. They are often informal mentors — the practitioner that newer adopters learn from, directly or through their public writing.

DevRel's role with Mechanics is to provide the depth they need and to create the conditions in which their expertise becomes visible to the wider ecosystem. A Mechanic who feels heard and respected is one of the most powerful signals of ecosystem health.
