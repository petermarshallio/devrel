# The developer journey

The journey a practitioner takes from first encounter to active contributor
follows seven stages. Each stage has a characteristic question the practitioner
is asking, a characteristic set of needs, and characteristic failure modes that
cause practitioners to stall or leave.

| Stage | The practitioner's question |
| --- | --- |
| [Attraction](attraction.md) | Is there something here worth paying attention to? |
| [Awareness](awareness.md) | What is this, and is it relevant to me? |
| [Assessment](assessment.md) | Should I use this? Can I? |
| [Adoption](adoption.md) | I am building with this. |
| [Acclimation](acclimation.md) | I am running this in production. I am finding my feet. |
| [Application](application.md) | I am getting real value from this at scale. |
| [Advocacy](advocacy.md) | I want others to know about this. I want to contribute. |

The stages are not a funnel — practitioners do not drop out cleanly at one stage
and disappear. They stall, plateau, and sometimes regress. The practitioner who
reached Application with one use case and is now starting a second one is
effectively re-entering at Assessment. The practitioner who has been at
Application for three years without engaging with the ecosystem is not at
Advocacy — they are at Application, permanently.

The journey also maps onto the software development lifecycle. The
pre-production phases — Plan, Code, Build, Test — correspond broadly to
Attraction through Adoption, where the
[Expert Educator](../practitioners/expert-educator.md) is most at home. The
post-production phases — Release, Deploy, Operate, Monitor — correspond to
Acclimation through Advocacy, where the
[Connected Correspondent](../practitioners/connected-correspondent.md) is most
effective. The [Reputable Resonator](../practitioners/reputable-resonator.md)
operates across both ends: inspiring entry at Attraction and Awareness, and
amplifying Guardians back into the ecosystem at Advocacy.

The journey belongs to everyone who builds with technology — the senior engineer
and the bootcamp graduate, the enterprise architect and the solo founder, the
practitioner at a Fortune 500 and the one at a startup that does not yet have a
name. DevRel that only sees the visible, already-credentialled practitioner will
miss the people who most need its attention and who will, in time, become its
most valuable Guardians.

> Never forget the little students, the startups, the graduates, the
> under-represented groups. From terminal to terminal they go. —
> [Wesley Faulkner](https://www.linkedin.com/in/wesley83/), DevRelCon London,
> 2023
