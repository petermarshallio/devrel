# Developer Relations

**What makes a developer trust a technology enough to build their career on it?
What makes them come back when something breaks at 2am, share what they learned,
help the person who asked the same question they asked six months ago?**

**It is not marketing. It is not support. It is something older and more human
than either — the slow accumulation of knowledge, trust, and belonging that
turns a user into a practitioner, and a practitioner into someone who gives
back.**

**_Developer Relations is the organisational capability that tends that process
deliberately._**

## What DevRel is

_Developer Relations is about the masterful management of relationships._

It increases capability, confidence, and autonomy - not just of individuals, but
of a network of connected individuals.

When it works well, it reduces uncertainty, friction, and the cognitive cost of
adopting new ways of doing something, forming resilient relationships that
inspires self-organizing social action.

It asks _how can we create a super-cool party where people will thrive and
connect?_

Developer Relations teams are in charge of their own _social network_. They have
their own _graph_. The nodes are practitioners, organisations, and products. The
edges are personal relationships, task collaborations. It's a marketplace for
value exchange.

It must see that graph as a whole. It must tend it to it like a farm: where are
people isolated? where are connections weak? They leave no stone unturned
looking for ways to multiply and strengthen those connections, inspiring
practitioners to connect directly with each other, to give back, and to remove
risks posed by superheros and monopolies.

A conference hallway conversation, a forum thread answered at 2am, years of
co-authoring integrations. Some edges are strong, some are weak, but to DevRel
they all matter.

The company DevRel belongs to has goals. And the community they make have goals,
too. When those goals align, DevRel excels and accelerates that alignment. When
they diverge, DevRel the tension can be terrifying.

This makes it a hard job: to understand both sides of the coin, and to design
programs, relationships, and interventions that operate in the alignment zone as
much as possible. They are, very often, diplomats afraid of excommunication.
Diplomats who believe in these three things:

- Make (other) people the hero.
- Connect the dots.
- Strengthen the network.

DevRel might make demos, but their demos are about the graph. Is the new
language pack gonna help everyone in France use the product? Sure! Here's
François (in French) showing you how to install it. Why not come to his meetup
for a pizza? The party is open, the invitations are sent out, everyone's gonna
be installing the French language pack. You wanna sell them the German pack for
$5? Sure! Come along! But this is our house - the in our house the rules are
simple.

Or, like an invasive species in a corn field, you'll get weeded out...
